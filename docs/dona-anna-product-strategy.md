# Doña Anna Product Strategy

## Core positioning

Doña Anna should be built around timing, measurement and origin:

- early harvest for stronger phenolic profile
- two clearly separated polyphenol levels
- batch transparency through Olivia OS
- old-tree scarcity only where it creates measurable or sensory value
- monovarietal releases only after test pressing confirms a distinct taste story

## Recommended product ladder

1. **Doña Anna Verde Vivo**
   - First early harvest.
   - Highest bitterness, strongest pepper finish, lowest oil yield.
   - Premium 250 ml, numbered bottles, lab result printed or QR-linked.

2. **Doña Anna Verde Alto**
   - Second early harvest.
   - Still high-polyphenol, but rounder and easier for daily use.
   - 250 ml and 500 ml; likely the best commercial flagship.

3. **Doña Anna Raíz Antigua**
   - Old-tree estate selection.
   - Start as one blended old-tree batch.
   - Split by variety only if tests show a clear sensory or analytical advantage.

4. **Doña Anna Monovarietal**
   - Genovesa, Gordal/Gordial, Changlot Real, Picual.
   - Use for tasting packs, restaurants, education and collectors.
   - Avoid too many permanent SKUs until demand is proven.

5. **Doña Anna Chef / Cocina Viva**
   - Larger bottle or tin for restaurants and premium home kitchens.
   - Not necessarily the highest polyphenol line, but still estate traceable.

6. **Doña Anna Mesa**
   - Table olives, especially if Gordal/Gordial has strong eating quality.
   - Separate from oil branding, but same visual system.

## Label principle

Every front label should show:

- brand: Doña Anna
- product line: Verde Vivo / Verde Alto / Raíz Antigua / Monovarietal
- harvest window
- batch number
- bottle number if limited
- QR to Olivia trace page

Avoid medical language. Use verified lab numbers and the permitted EU wording only when the oil qualifies.
